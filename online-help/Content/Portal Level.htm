Portal Level

A portal's level is the average level of its resonator slots, including empty ones (as level 0), rounded down. The only exception to the rounding rule is that a claimed portal can never be below level 1 (e.g. the average resonator level of a portal with a single level 1 resonator on it is 0.13, but it's still be a level 1 portal).

Effects

Portal level effects the level of gear obtained when the portal is hacked, the number of glyphs in its glyph hacking sequences, and the amount of XM it drains from enemy agents who hack it.

Gear Level

If an agent's level is equivalent to or greater than the level of a portal, hacking it gives them gear roughly the same as the level of the portal. If the agent's level is lower than the level of the portal, it will give them gear of roughly their level. The type and amount of gear granted is also affected by whether the agent is the same faction as the portal.

Machina portals, regardless of their level, always drop gear as if they were a level 1 portal.

Glyph Sequences

See also: How To Glyph Hack

Glyph hacking sequences can contain one to five glyphs. Higher level portals give longer glyph sequences, which can give increased bonus gear and AP, and more points on the translator medal.

Misconceptions

Portal level does not directly effect the length of links that can be thrown from the portal, but the level of resonators on the portal does, so they're roughly correlated.

See also: How to Create Links

Higher level portals are not tougher than lower level portals. Higher level resonators hold more XM, so if they're fully charged it takes more resources for an enemy agent to drain them, but they aren't any more resistant to damage. The only way to make a portal tougher is by using mods.

Building High Level Portals

Because one agent can only deploy a certain number of each level of resonator, it takes multiple agents to make higher level portals.
