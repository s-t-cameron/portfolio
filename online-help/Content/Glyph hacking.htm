How to Glyph Hack

Glyph hacking is an advanced form of portal hacking and memorization mini-game. If a glyph hack is completed successfully, you receive more items and AP than you'd receive from a regular hack.

After initiating a glyph hack, you're shown a series of glyphs and given a countdown, during which you must enter the glyphs exactly as they were displayed.

Each glyph is drawn with one continuous stroke that connects dots on an eleven dot grid. Glyphs translate directly into English words or concepts, and glyph sequences string them together to construct short thematic messages.

Glyph Hacking

There are four phases to a glyph hack: the command channel, receiving and memorizing the glyph sequence, repeating back the glyph sequence, and results.

Note: Hitting the "x" at the bottom of the screen at any time will immediately cancel the glyph hack, granting you the same results as a standard hack with no consequences.

  1. Initiate the glyph hack by long pressing on the Hack button until three smaller buttons appear. Drag your finger up to the leftmost button, which resembles a simplified glyph grid.

  2. As the glyph hack initializes, the command channel displays for a few seconds.

    This is an optional step, allowing you to enter glyphs that alter the difficulty and potential key output of the hack. While the message "Command channel open..." is displayed, you can enter any of these four glyphs:
    Command Glyph Translation Effect
    Complex Speed up the display of the glyph sequence. This does not affect the input timer or hack output.
    Simple Slow down the display of the glyph sequence. This does not affect the input timer or hack output.
    More

    Request a key, even if you're already holding one. This has the same likelihood to grant a key as a standard hack, but may grant up to three keys rather than just one.
    Less Request no key.

    You can combine command glyphs as long as their effects aren't mutually exclusive.

    Valid Command Glyph Combinations
    MORE, COMPLEX LESS, COMPLEX
    MORE, SIMPLE LESS, SIMPLE

  3. The command channel closes automatically and you receive the glyph sequence you have to memorize.

    Note: The number of glyphs displayed is based on the portal level and your agent level. You can never receive a glyph sequence more complex than your level.

    Glyphs show up one at a time, only staying on the screen briefly.

  4. When the countdown timer begins, enter the glyphs you were shown in the order you were shown them.

    Note: If you make a mistake, you can undo the glyphs you've entered one at a time by hitting "redo".

    Each glyph must be drawn in one continuous motion, but it doesn't matter which end you start at. Make sure to hit every dot-- the path between them is less important than the dots the glyph contains.

    Accuracy is more important than pace. Bonuses are rewarded based on speed, but only if every glyph is entered correctly.

  5. When you've entered an attempt at each glyph in the sequence, or the timer runs out, the resolution screen appears. This shows you the glyph sequence again, highlighting your correct inputs in yellow and your incorrect inputs in red, and displays your overall hacking and speed bonus.

  6. The gear granted for the standard hack will pop up in one window, then any bonus gear from the glyph hack will pop up in a second window.
